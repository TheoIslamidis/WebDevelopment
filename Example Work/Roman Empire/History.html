The Rise of Rome

The history of the Roman Empire begins with the legendary founding of Rome by Romulus and Remus in 753 BC. Over centuries, Rome evolved from a small city-state to a dominant Mediterranean power.

Key events during the rise of Rome include the establishment of the Roman Republic in 509 BC, the Punic Wars with Carthage, and the conquest of Greece and Egypt.

The Roman Republic

The Roman Republic was characterized by a system of government where elected officials served fixed terms. It saw significant expansion of Roman territories and the spread of Roman culture.

However, internal conflicts, such as the struggle between the patricians and the plebeians, and the rise of powerful generals like Julius Caesar, eventually led to the downfall of the Republic.

The Roman Empire

Julius Caesar's assassination in 44 BC marked the end of the Roman Republic and the beginning of the Roman Empire. His adopted heir, Octavian (later known as Augustus), became the first Roman Emperor.

The Roman Empire reached its peak during the reign of Emperor Trajan in the 2nd century AD, encompassing vast territories from Britain to Mesopotamia.
